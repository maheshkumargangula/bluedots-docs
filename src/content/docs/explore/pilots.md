Two district pilots — in **Ghaziabad** (Uttar Pradesh) and **Dharwad** (Karnataka) — show that the local discovery crisis is solvable in practice.

## What changed

### Job visibility & market legibility

| | Before | With Blue Dots |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Jobs visible on national platforms | < 100 | **10,000+** (from < 10% of SMBs, via Voice AI) |
| Cost to surface local intelligence | ₹500 / survey | **₹10 / interaction** |

### Speed & proximity of connection

| | Before | With Blue Dots |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Discovery time | > 2 weeks | **< 1 day** |
| Talent source | Another town/city | **< 2 bus stops** |

### Placement & economic outcomes

| | Before | With Blue Dots |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Placement / conversion | < 10% (broad job fairs) | **> 50%** (targeted drives) |
| Economic effect | Long-distance migration | **Local multiplier** — income & spending stay in the district |

## Why it worked

- **Voice-AI capture** made it economically viable to surface signals from a long tail of small businesses that surveys never reach.
- **Proximity-aware matching** connected people already within bus-stop distance.
- **Aggregator-led onboarding** built signal density fast enough for matches to actually occur.

## What it implies

The pilots are a template for the [Adaptor Onboarding](/bluedots-docs/guides/adaptor-onboarding/) path: stand up Signals, onboard via aggregators, capture by voice, run a bounded district pilot, and measure the same before/after metrics.
